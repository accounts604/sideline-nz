---

## 1. Reframe the offer (this is the key insight)

You have **three different offers**, and they should not be weighted equally:

1. **Custom Orders** → low–mid ticket, frequent, transactional
2. **Team Stores (revenue share)** → **high ticket, long-term partnership**
3. **Supporter / club gear** → downstream upside

So the mistake would be:
❌ “Team Stores = core CTA for everyone”

The *correct* positioning is:
✅ **Team Stores = premium partnership offer for the right clubs**

That means:

* Still visible on the homepage
* But **sold, not browsed**
* Framed as *“for established clubs & schools”*

---

## 2. What Team Stores should be on the homepage

Not a shop.
Not a directory.
Not “find your store” first.

It should be positioned as:

> **“Want us to run your club’s online store and share revenue back?”**

That’s a very different sell.

---

## 3. Where it sits in the homepage hierarchy (important)

### Recommended order (updated)

1. Hero – “Uniforms made simple”
2. Trust strip – logos
3. **Custom Orders / Start a Project** (primary CTA)
4. **Team Stores – Partnership Model** (secondary, premium)
5. How it Works
6. Testimonials
7. Final CTA

This does two things:

* Keeps your volume funnel clean
* Signals Team Stores as *selective and valuable*

---

## 4. Homepage Team Stores section – revised positioning

### Section headline (this matters a lot)

Pick something that implies **selectivity + upside**:

* **“Team stores that fund your club.”**
* **“Turn supporter gear into club revenue.”**
* **“A managed team store — with money back to your club.”**

This immediately filters the right audience.

---

### Supporting copy (tight + honest)

> For selected clubs and schools, we set up and manage a dedicated online team store — handling orders, payments, and delivery, while returning a portion of sales back to your organisation.

This frames it as:

* Managed
* Ongoing
* Partnership-based
* Not for everyone

---

## 5. How to visually signal “high-ticket / partnership”

### DO

* Use a **darker or richer colour break** (charcoal, deep green, navy)
* Fewer cards, more whitespace
* Emphasise outcomes, not features

### DON’T

* List lots of stores
* Make it feel like a marketplace
* Put “Find your store” as the main CTA

This is not Dynasty’s public directory energy — it’s **enterprise-lite**.

---

## 6. CTA strategy (this is critical)

You only need **one CTA** here:

### Primary CTA

**`Apply for a Team Store`**

Secondary (small text link):

* “Already have a store? View stores →”

Why:

* “Apply” signals qualification
* Protects your time
* Reinforces high-ticket nature
