Fix the homepage hero carousel sizing, image scaling, and section spacing.

We have 7 hero images. All have pure black backgrounds and are portrait 
orientation (~3:4 ratio). The hero layout places text on the left, 
image on the right. The goal is visual consistency across all slides 
so every image feels equally impactful.

────────────────────────────────────────
1. HERO SECTION — GLOBAL SETTINGS
────────────────────────────────────────
- Hero height: 92vh on desktop, 100svh on mobile
- Background: #000000 (matches all image backgrounds — seamless blend)
- Image container: position absolute or relative, anchored bottom-right
- Image should display full height of the hero section
- Use object-fit: contain with bottom alignment so figures are 
  grounded at the bottom edge
- Image max-height: 100% of hero section height
- Image max-width: 55% on desktop (right half of screen)

────────────────────────────────────────
2. IMAGE-SPECIFIC OVERRIDES
────────────────────────────────────────
Apply slide-specific CSS classes or data attributes for these overrides:

ACCESSORIES SLIDE (two people, beanie + cap):
- The subjects sit in the LOWER 60% of the image with heavy black 
  space above. To compensate:
  - Apply transform: scale(1.4) on the image
  - Apply transform-origin: bottom center
  - This pushes the figures up to fill the frame consistently
  - Do NOT crop — scale only

NETBALL ACTION SHOT (arm raised, portrait):
- This image is a tighter portrait crop and the subject extends 
  beyond normal frame
- Apply object-position: center top to anchor from the top
- Scale to: 1.1 if needed to fill vertical space

ALL OTHER SLIDES (Rugby, Tracksuit, Gridiron, Basketball, Windbreaker):
- No scaling override needed — these fill the frame well
- object-fit: contain, object-position: bottom right
